Georgian Culture

Discover the rich traditions, customs, and cultural nuances of Georgia. Understanding local culture will enrich your expat experience and help you connect with Georgians.

Georgian Supra (Feast)

Traditional Georgian feast with elaborate toasts, wine, and hospitality.

Cultural Importance:

Central to Georgian social life

Tips for Expats:

Accept invitations graciously, participate in toasts, pace yourself with wine

Tamada (Toastmaster)

The person who leads toasts at Georgian feasts and celebrations.

Cultural Importance:

Highly respected role in Georgian culture

Tips for Expats:

Listen respectfully to toasts, wait for your turn to speak

Georgian Hospitality

Guests are considered a gift from God in Georgian culture.

Cultural Importance:

Core value of Georgian society

Tips for Expats:

Accept hospitality graciously, bring small gifts when visiting homes

Orthodox Christianity

Deeply rooted religious traditions and customs.

Cultural Importance:

Influences daily life and cultural practices

Tips for Expats:

Dress modestly in churches, respect religious holidays and customs

Cultural Etiquette

Social Interactions

  • Greet with a handshake or kiss on both cheeks
  • Maintain eye contact during conversations
  • Show respect to elders
  • Remove shoes when entering homes

Dining Etiquette

  • Wait for the host to start eating
  • Keep hands visible on the table
  • Finish everything on your plate
  • Participate in toasts when offered

Cultural Immersion Tips

Do:

  • • Learn basic Georgian phrases
  • • Attend local festivals and events
  • • Try traditional Georgian food
  • • Show interest in Georgian history
  • • Respect religious customs

Avoid:

  • • Comparing Georgia negatively to other countries
  • • Refusing hospitality or food offers
  • • Discussing sensitive political topics
  • • Wearing revealing clothing in religious sites
  • • Being impatient with Georgian time concepts
